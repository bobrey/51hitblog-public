苹果推送通知－实例

  <p>
     
  </p>
  
  <p>
     
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <span>　正常的iPhone刷系统之后，是没有设备证书和密钥的。这就是为什么iPhone会需要连接到 iTunes上进行激活——激活过程中，Apple会分配给每台iPhone独一无二的设备证书(device certificate)和密钥(key)。(本人由于没有意思到这个 一直收到 &#8220;未找到应用程序的&#8221;aps-environment&#8221; 的权利字符串&#8221;)</span>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <span><br /> </br></span>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    One of the key limitations of the iPhone is its constraint on running applications in the background. Because of this, applications cannot keep running in the background when the user switches to another application. So, applications that require a constant<br /> state of connectivity (such as social networking applications) will not be able to receive timely updates.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    To remedy this limitation, Apple released the Apple Push Notification Service (APNs). The APNs is a service that allows your device to be constantly connected to Apple&#8217;s push notification server. When you want to send a push notification to an application<br /> installed on the users&#8217; devices, you (<em>the provider</em>) can contact the APNs so that it can deliver a push message to the particular application installed on the intended device.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    In this article, you will learn how to perform the various steps needed to create an iPhone application that uses the APNs.
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Generating a Certificate Request
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    The first step to using the APNs is to generate a certificate request file so that you can use it to request for a development SSL certificate later on.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> Launch the Keychain Access application in your Mac OS X.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> Select Keychain Access&#8217;Certificate Assistant&#8217;Request a Certificate From a Certificate Authority (see Figure 1):
  </p>
  
  <p>
     
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="1" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-1.jpg?resize=500%2C201"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 1</strong>. Generating a certificate request</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong></strong>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> Enter the information required and check the Saved to disk option. Click Continue (see Figure 2).
  </p>
  
  <p>
     
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="2" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-2.jpg?resize=500%2C362" data-recalc-dims="1" />

  <h2>
    Creating an App ID
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    Each iPhone applications that uses the APNs must have a unique application ID that uniquely identifies itself. In this step, you will learn how to create an App ID for push notification.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> Sign in to the iPhone Developer Program at: <a href="http://developer.apple.com/iphone/">http://developer.apple.com/iphone/</a>. Click on the iPhone Developer Program Portal on the right of the page (see Figure 4).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="4" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-4.jpg?resize=640%2C206"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 4</strong>. Launching the iPhone Developer Program Portal</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> You should see the welcome page (see Figure 5).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="5" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-5.jpg?resize=600%2C370"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 5</strong>. The welcome screen of the iPhone Developer Program Portal</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> Click on the App IDs tab on the left and then click on the New App ID button (see Figure 6).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="6" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-6.jpg?resize=640%2C220"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 6</strong>. Clicking on the App ID tab</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>4.</strong> Enter &#8220;PushAppID&#8221; for the Description and select Generate New for the Bundle Seed ID. For the Bundle Identifier, enter <span><code>net.learn2develop.MyPushApp</code></span>. Click Submit (see Figure 7).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="7" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-7.jpg?resize=640%2C433"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 7</strong>. Creating a new App ID</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>5.</strong> You should now see the App ID that you have created (together with those you have previously created) (see Figure 8).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="8" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-8.jpg?resize=500%2C84"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 8</strong>. Viewing the newly created App ID</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Configuring an App ID for Push Notifications
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    Once an App ID is created, you need to configure it for push notifications.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> To configure an App ID for push notification, you need to click the Configure link displayed to the right of the App ID. You will now see the option (see Figure 9).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="9" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-9.jpg?resize=600%2C257"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 9</strong>. Configuring an App ID for push notification service</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Check the Enable for Apple Push Notification service option and click the Configure button displayed to the right of the Development Push SSL Certificate.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> You will now see the Apple Push Notification service SSL Certificate Assistant screen. Click Continue (see Figure 10).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="10" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-10.jpg?resize=500%2C457"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 10</strong>. The Apple Push Notification service SSL Certificate Assistant screen</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> Click the Choose File button to locate the Certificate Request file that you have saved earlier. Click Generate (see Figure 11).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="11" src="https://i0.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-11.jpg?resize=500%2C457"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 11</strong>. Generating the SSL certificate</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>4.</strong> Your SSL Certificate will now be generated. Click Continue (see Figure 12).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="12" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-12.jpg?resize=500%2C457"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 12</strong>. The APNs SSL certificate that is generated</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>5.</strong> Click the Download Now button to download the SSL Certificate. Click Done (see Figure 13).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="13" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-13.jpg?resize=500%2C457"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 13</strong>. Downloading the certificate generated</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>6.</strong> The SSL Certificate that you download is named <span><code>aps.developer.identity.cer</code></span>. Double-click on it to install it in the Keychain Access application (see Figure 14). The SSL certificate will be used by your provider<br /> application so that it can contact the APNs to send push notifications to your applications.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="14" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-14.jpg?resize=500%2C301"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 14</strong>. Installing the generated certificate into the Keychain Access application</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Creating a Provisioning Profile
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    The next step is to create a provisioning profile so that your application can be installed onto a real device.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> Back in the iPhone Development Program Portal, click on the Provisioning tab and click on the New Profile button (see Figure 15).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="15" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-15.jpg?resize=640%2C159"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 15</strong>. Selecting the Provisioning tab</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> Type in <span><code>MyDevicesProfile</code></span> as the profile name. Select PushAppID as the App ID. Finally, check all the devices that you want to provision (you can register these devices with the iPhone Developer Program Portal<br /> through the Devices tab). Click Submit (see Figure 16).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="16" src="https://i2.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-16.jpg?resize=640%2C363"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 16</strong>. Creating a new provisioning profile</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> The provisioning profile will now be pending approval. After a while, you will see it appear. Click on the Download button to download the provisioning profile (see Figure 17).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="17" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-17.jpg?resize=600%2C176"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 17</strong>. Pending the approval of the provisioning profile</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>4.</strong> The downloaded provisioning profile is named MyDevicesProfile.mobileprovision.
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Provisioning a Device
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    With the provision profile created, you will now install it onto a real device.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> Connect your iPhone or iPod Touch to your Mac.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> Drag and drop the downloaded <span><code>MyDevicesProfile.mobileprovision</code></span> file onto the Xcode icon on the Dock.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> Launch the Organizer application from within Xcode and select the device currently connected to your Mac. You should see the <span><code>MyDevicesProfile</code></span> installed on the device (see Figure 18).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="18" src="https://i0.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-18.jpg?resize=600%2C343"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 18</strong>. Viewing the installed provisioning profile</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Creating the iPhone Application
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    <strong>1.</strong> In Xcode, create a new View-Based Application project and name it as ApplePushNotification.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>2.</strong> Drag and drop a WAV file (shown as <span><code>beep.wav</code></span> in this example) onto the Resources folder in Xcode (see Figure 19).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="19" src="https://mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-19.jpg?resize=355%2C307"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 19</strong>. Adding a WAV file to the project</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <strong>3.</strong> Expand on the Targets item in Xcode and select the ApplePushNotification item. Press Command-I. In the Info window, click the Properties tab (see Figure 20).
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img alt="20" src="https://i0.wp.com/mobiforge.com/files/iphone-push-20.jpg?resize=600%2C489"  data-recalc-dims="1" /><br /> <strong>Figure 20</strong>. Entering the App ID for the application</br></img>
  </p>
  
  <p>
    In the Identifier textbox, type <<span><code>net.learn2develop.MyPushApp</code></span>.

  <p>
